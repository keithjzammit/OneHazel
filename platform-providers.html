THE PROBLEM

Your operators need integrations. You should not be building them.

Every operator wants something different

Operator A needs fraud detection from Vendor X. Operator B wants analytics from Vendor Y. Operator C needs both, plus a compliance tool you have never heard of. Multiply that across your entire portfolio and integration requests become your biggest engineering bottleneck.

Custom integrations do not scale

Building one-off integrations for individual operators is expensive, slow, and creates a maintenance burden that grows with every new connection. Your engineering team should be building your platform, not maintaining API plumbing.

Vendor fragmentation is getting worse

The number of specialised AI and data vendors in every vertical is exploding. Your operators expect you to keep pace. Without a scalable integration strategy, you lose operators to platforms that connect to the tools they need.

THE SOLUTION

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Who qualifies as a platform provider?

Any company that serves multiple operators through a shared platform -- white-label solutions, aggregation platforms, managed services providers, or any business where multiple end-clients operate on your infrastructure.

How is this different from an Enterprise plan?

The Enterprise plan is for a single organisation managing their own integrations. The Platform Provider programme is for companies that serve a network of operators and want to offer integration capabilities across that entire network under one agreement with centralised billing and multi-tenant architecture.

Do my operators need their own OneHazel accounts?

No. Operators access the integration marketplace through your platform. Each operator gets an isolated workspace, but everything is managed under your platform provider agreement. No separate subscriptions, no separate billing.

Can I white-label the marketplace?

Yes. The integration marketplace can be embedded into your platform with your branding. Operators interact with your UI -- OneHazel powers the backend. Full white-label options are available on all platform provider agreements.

How does pricing work?

Platform provider pricing is volume-based and tailored to your network size. One agreement covers all operators. The more operators you bring, the lower the per-operator cost. Contact our platform team for a proposal.

How long does deployment take?

Typical deployment takes 2--4 weeks from scoping to live operators. This includes API integration, marketplace configuration, operator workspace setup, and initial connector activation. Dedicated onboarding support is included.

What happens when I add new operators?

New operators are provisioned instantly through the API or your platform dashboard. They immediately get access to the full vendor marketplace with their own isolated workspace. No additional setup from OneHazel's side.

Can my operators request specific vendors?

Yes. Operator vendor requests flow through to OneHazel's AI agents, which can build new connectors in 3--7 days. This is included in the platform provider agreement -- no additional charges for standard connector builds.
