The Integration Layer for Energy & Utilities

Energy companies operate across two worlds — operational technology (SCADA, historians, PLCs) and information technology (ERP, CMMS, compliance systems). These worlds speak different protocols, sit on different networks, and are managed by different teams. Bridging them is the industry's most critical integration challenge.

Why Energy Integration Is Uniquely Hard

Energy and utilities face integration challenges that no horizontal platform was built to solve. Three structural problems define the landscape.

OT/IT Divide

Operational systems use industrial protocols (Modbus, DNP3, OPC-UA) in secured, often air-gapped networks. Business systems use REST APIs in the cloud. Generic automation platforms like Zapier and Make.com exist entirely in the IT world — they cannot reach operational technology.

Regulatory Burden

NERC CIP for utilities, EPA emissions reporting, FERC filings, IEC 62443 for industrial cybersecurity — compliance requires operational data to flow to reporting systems with full audit trails. Manual compilation is error-prone and expensive.

Legacy at Scale

Many facilities run 20–30 year old DCS and SCADA systems alongside modern cloud analytics. The integration challenge isn't just technical — it's bridging decades of technology evolution within a single operation.
