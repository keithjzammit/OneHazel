Why Healthcare Integration Is Broken

The healthcare data ecosystem was never designed for interoperability. Three structural problems make integration harder here than in any other industry.

Protocol Fragmentation

Healthcare systems communicate via HL7v2, FHIR R4, X12 EDI, and proprietary REST APIs. Even two systems both claiming "FHIR support" implement it differently. No horizontal iPaaS handles these protocols.

Compliance Barriers

HIPAA, HITECH, and state-level privacy laws require BAAs, audit trails, and encryption for any system touching PHI. Generic automation platforms like Zapier cannot sign BAAs and are structurally incompatible with healthcare workflows.

Vendor Lock-in

Switching EHR vendors or adding a new telehealth platform means rebuilding integrations from scratch. Each connection is a custom, brittle point-to-point link maintained by scarce health IT staff.
